⚡
Generator
The original power source. Produces 480V / 277V three-phase electricity — a high-voltage format used for industrial and commercial buildings.
🗃️
Distribution Panel
Like a highway junction — it receives all the power from the generator and splits it into separate circuits, each protected by its own breaker.
🔒
Circuit Breaker
A safety device that automatically cuts power if there's a fault (overload or short circuit). Each one protects a different part of the system.
🔌
Disconnect Switch
A manual switch used by electricians to safely isolate a section of the system for maintenance, without touching live wires.
🔧
Transformer
Converts high voltage (480V) down to a lower, safer voltage (e.g. 208V or 120V) that standard equipment and outlets can use.
📋
Final Panel (A / B / D)
The last stop — the breaker panel you'd find on a wall in a room or floor. It distributes power to outlets, lights, and equipment.
